Contradictions

Some Major Findings

I’m seeing two primary contradictions emerge through the coding and analysis process. First, multiple participants stated keeping their tabs open as a means of setting reminders for themselves or storing away information. However, the tabs did more than remind participants of other tasks, but seemed to distract them from their current tasks as well, with Participant 5 describing open tabs as “noise”. I’m beginning to think that there may be some temporality associated with when participants would like to be able to see their open tabs as reminders, something I could continue to explore. The other contradiction is that while participants seem adamant that tabs will continue to serve a function at a future time – and thus need to remain open, it was hard for them to come up with times that going back to something was useful or instances in which something significant was lost when tabs did inadvertently close. Contradictions indicate a difference between participant behaviors and their perceptions of the behaviors.
